# MARU Design Direction

## Design Context

### Users

MARU serves affluent customers who expect a premium retail experience and choose objects as part of how they shape a home, not only for utility. The first design pass assumes three representative purchase situations:

1. Completing a new home or renovated room with a considered set of furniture and objects.
2. Choosing one signature object that makes an everyday space feel personal and elevated.
3. Selecting a formal housewarming, anniversary, or milestone gift with confidence in presentation and service.

These customers value discernment, material quality, calm guidance, delivery certainty, and discreet service. They should never feel hurried by dense promotions or a marketplace-like volume of choices.

### Brand Personality

The user-defined brand qualities are luxury, comfort, and coziness. In visual and interaction terms, MARU expresses them as:

- **Tailored** — precise composition, impeccable alignment, confident editing, and no decorative excess.
- **Hushed** — quiet hierarchy, restrained color, generous negative space, and calm motion.
- **Tactile** — material-led photography, warm surfaces, human-scale interiors, and copy that makes ownership feel lived-in.

The emotional outcome is quiet confidence: the customer feels understood, unpressured, and certain that every detail has been considered.

### Aesthetic Direction

MARU is a light-dominant luxury retail environment inspired by a private residential gallery. The interface uses warm ivory rather than pure white, softened ink rather than pure black, editorial imagery at architectural scale, and a single deep oxblood accent for decisive actions. Korean display typography carries warmth and tactility; compact sans-serif typography keeps commerce and service information precise.

Reference roles:

- Fendi Casa: material atmosphere and complete residential scenes.
- Hermès: restraint, whitespace, and quiet global navigation.
- Louis Vuitton: campaign-first hierarchy and visible client service.
- guud/casamia: familiar Korean commerce navigation and category clarity.

MARU will not copy reference layouts, logos, imagery, or signature brand devices. It will derive only general principles of hierarchy, rhythm, service, and material presentation.

Anti-references:

- Generic SaaS dashboards, floating card mosaics, and pill-heavy navigation.
- Faux luxury expressed through black-and-gold clichés, gold gradients, or ornamental serif overload.
- Beige boutique templates with weak hierarchy and indistinguishable sections.
- Glassmorphism, neon effects, gradient text, and decorative motion.
- Promotion-led marketplace density, repeated product shelves, and urgency tactics.

### Design Principles

1. **Scene before SKU.** Establish atmosphere and use context before presenting a tightly edited product selection.
2. **Restraint signals value.** Fewer elements, fewer badges, and fewer competing actions make each decision feel deliberate.
3. **Warmth comes from material.** Photography, typography, spacing, and copy create coziness; ornamental effects do not.
4. **Service is part of luxury.** Delivery, returns, consultation, order care, and support remain visible at moments of uncertainty.
5. **Commerce stays legible.** Price, availability, variants, cart state, and checkout progress must remain immediate and accessible.
6. **Admin is an operations product.** The admin experience is neutral, information-dense, precise, and free of campaign-style hero sections.
7. **Accessibility is invisible craft.** WCAG AA contrast, keyboard operation, reduced motion, and clear focus states are baseline quality.

## Visual Thesis

MARU should feel like entering a hushed residential gallery on a bright afternoon: architectural images establish the room, concise editorial language guides attention, and carefully spaced objects invite a closer look. The visual system pairs a warm Korean serif display face with a disciplined Korean sans-serif, uses hairline dividers and squared or subtly softened geometry, and reserves its oxblood accent for moments of commitment.

## Content Plan

The storefront home page follows one clear narrative:

1. **Brand world** — one immersive interior scene, one proposition, one primary collection action.
2. **Ways to live** — three editorial category entries framed as rooms or rituals rather than inventory departments.
3. **Curated collection** — a limited product edit with price and essential commerce information.
4. **Material story** — an editorial feature that explains craft, comfort, or provenance.
5. **New arrivals and service** — a concise final product edit followed by delivery, consultation, and care assurances.

Search, category, and product pages preserve this editorial calm while increasing information density according to task. Cart and checkout remove discovery distractions and foreground certainty, totals, fulfillment, and support.

## Interaction Thesis

Interactions are quiet, fast, and physically grounded. Images reveal with subtle opacity and scale, navigation and filters use short fades or slides, and product actions surface only when useful. Motion stays within 140–240ms for controls, uses no continuous decoration, and is disabled or simplified for `prefers-reduced-motion`. Hover never carries information that keyboard and touch users cannot access.

## Theme and Token Intent

- Light-dominant storefront; neutral light admin workspace.
- Warm ivory canvas, linen secondary surface, softened ink text, muted stone borders, deep oxblood accent.
- No pure `#000000` or `#ffffff`; use OKLCH tokens with tested accessible pairings.
- Two type families maximum: one Korean-capable serif for display and one Korean-capable sans-serif for interface and body.
- Radius is functional and restrained: small control radius, medium media radius, no default pill containers.
- Spacing uses a consistent 4px-derived scale with generous section rhythm and compact transaction density.
